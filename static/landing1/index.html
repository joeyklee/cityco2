Urban CO2 Atlas

Mobile Mapping Project

1. Introduction

Cities are a major influence in climate change. The are responsible for up to 70% of co2 emissions world wide [reference].
But cities also have the highest potential to become green. We don’t have to rely on cars, have only short ways to our workplace and food is distributed more easily.
So what is there still missing? We don’t have the tools yet to find out where in our cities the problems are. If you want to fix your house, you have to know where what’s wrong with it.
The same applies for our cities….

2. Building the tools

Traditional methods operate poorly – they are sparsely located fixed points spread across [something] or are measured from space over thousands of kilometres above you with coarse resolutions.

Given improvements in technology and the growth of the opensource community, new opportunities exist to help benchmarch our emissions in our communities. Using a mobile approach to sensing and monitoring emissions, we can use cars and bikes to measure greenhouse gas concentrations at a local level/neighborhood level.

At UBC we have developed new methods to improve the situation.
